Orbit Camera2

Overview

The Orbit Camera prefab allows users to control a camera that orbits around a specified target. It supports both mouse and touch controls for panning, rotating, and zooming. This component is designed for intuitive camera navigation, making it ideal for 3D scene exploration or interactive environments .

Inspector Properties

Camera Settings

Affected Camera: The camera that will be controlled by this component.
Target to Look At: The object the camera will orbit around.

Control Options

Controls: Specifies the input methods for controlling the camera. Options:
  • Mouse: Allows camera control using a mouse.
  • Touch: Enables touch gestures for camera control.

Initial Distance and Rotation

Distance to Target: Sets the starting distance between the camera and the target. This value is clamped by Minimum Zoom Distance and Maximum Zoom Distance.
Y-Axis Value: The initial rotation angle around the vertical (Y) axis.
X-Axis Value: The initial rotation angle around the horizontal (X) axis.

Zoom Settings

Zoom Speed: The speed at which the camera zooms in and out.
Minimum Zoom Distance: The closest the camera can get to the target.
Maximum Zoom Distance: The farthest the camera can be from the target.

How to Use

  1. Attach the SimplifiedOrbitCameraMovement script to a GameObject in your scene.
  2. Assign a camera to the Affected Camera property in the Inspector.
  3. Set a target object in the Target to Look At field. This is the object the camera will orbit around.
  4. Configure the initial distance, rotation, and zoom settings to suit your scene.
  5. Select the desired input controls (Mouse, Touch, or both).
  6. Activate the Camera view in the toolbar to place the camera on the right position.

Additional Instructions

The Orbit Camera component provides the following functionality:

  • Rotation: Use the mouse or touch gestures to rotate the camera around the target.
  • Zoom: Scroll the mouse wheel or pinch to zoom in and out.
  • Panning: Drag the camera view using the right mouse button or a single touch gesture.

Best Practices

  • Ensure the target object is not null, as the camera orbits around this point.
  • For touch controls, test the functionality on a device that supports touch input.
  • Use the Minimum and Maximum Zoom Distance to prevent the camera from getting too close or too far from the target.
