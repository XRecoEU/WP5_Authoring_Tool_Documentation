3d Annotation

Overview

The 3d Annotation Prefab is designed to help users to place various user-specific annotations on their 3D models. These annotations can include images, videos, or ordanary text elements.

Inspector Properties

  • 3dAnnotationWithCanvas: Hold all your annotations and make it possible to see this directly in the play view.
  • 3dAnnotation: With this gameobject you can place the Annotations in a specific position in 3D world.
  • Collapsed: contains the button that is directly visible in the play mode. Once this button is clicked, it disappears, and the annotations contained in the Expanded gameobjects appears.
  • Expanded: contains all annotation objects, that will ne toggle by clicking on the collapsed button. You can insert images, videos or text objects.

How to Use

  1. Add the Prefab to your Scene via the prefab window.
  2. If there is no canvas added to your scene, you should first add the 3D annotation with canvas prefab to your scene so that your annotations are directly visible in play mode.
  3. Move the 3D annotation gameobject to the right position in your 3d scene.
  4. In the Collapsed gameobject you can change the text of the button and also insert a image to it.
  5. In the Expanded gameobject you can change the text of the annnotations and insert additional images, audio files or videos.

Editor Adjustments

To use the 3d Annotation Prefab in your scene you can use the following adjustments in the editor:

  • You can use the Attach To Cursor button in the lower right corner in the scene view to place the annotation directly on the 3d model.
  • You can use the Lightning Intensity scrollbar in the middle lower area of the scene view to adjust the lightning of your scene.

Additional Instructions

All of the following Instructions can only be executed in the Inspector Tab of the Unity Editor

  • To add Text to the Annotation component you can insert Text in the Anotation Text property Window.
  • To add Audio to the Annotation component click on the Annotation Audio property, a popup window will guide you to select a Audiofile from the Assets folder.
